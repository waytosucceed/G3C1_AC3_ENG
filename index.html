Nouns


Nouns are words that name people, places, things, or ideas. They help us identify and refer to specific entities in our language.

The different types of nouns are:

1. Proper noun: A proper noun identifies a specific person or place, such as a name.
Example: Mary, London, Sarah.

2. Common noun: Common nouns refer to general categories of people, things, or objects.
Example: teacher, city, book.

3. Collective noun: Collective nouns represent groups of people or things.
Example: team, flock, class.

4. Abstract noun: Abstract nouns denote qualities, actions, processes, ideas, or states.
Example: love, freedom, intelligence.

5. Concrete noun: Concrete nouns are tangible and can be perceived through the senses (sight, touch, hearing).
Example: apple, car, music.
